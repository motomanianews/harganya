Harga Kartu Tol Bali Mandara

Admin November 26, 2024

Harga Kartu Tol Bali Mandara - Are you planning to visit Bali and need to know the latest pricing information for the Bali Mandara Toll Road? Look no further! This comprehensive article will provide you with all the necessary information, from the history of the toll road to the current pricing structure and even some helpful tips for your travel needs.

Sejarah Berdirinya Jalan Tol Mandara Bali

The Bali Mandara Toll Road, also known as the Mandara Sembung Toll Road, is a 13.8-kilometer stretch of highway that connects the city of Denpasar to the port city of Benoa. The toll road was built to improve connectivity and reduce traffic congestion in the area. It was opened to the public in 2009 after a series of works that took about 6 years to complete. Since its opening, the toll road has become an essential artery for both locals and tourists alike.

Harga Kartu Tol Bali Mandara Saat Ini

As of[current year], the current toll pricing for the Bali Mandara Toll Road is as follows:

  • One-way fare for cars and sedimentangs (up to 3.5 tons): IDR 12,600
  • One-way fare for buses and trucks (3.5-7.5 tons): IDR 24,500
  • One-way fare for big trucks (above 7.5 tons): IDR 36,000
  • One-way fare for motorcycles: IDR 4,500
  • One-way fare for pedestrians and cyclists: IDR 1,000

Additionally, there is a discount of 5% for cash payments and 3% for electronic payment using e-cash or E-Wallet. It's also worth noting that some toll gates have designated lanes for electric vehicles, which require a special pass.

Tips Wisata Ke Bali Mandara

As a visitor to Bali, you may find the idea of navigating toll roads intimidating, But with these simple tips, you'll be well-prepared for your trip:

  1. Get familiar with the toll road map: Study the map beforehand to know which routes you should take and where the toll gates are located.
  2. Make sure you have cash: While electronic payment options are available, cash is still the most widely accepted payment method at this time.
  3. Consider purchasing a toll pass: If you plan to use the toll road frequently during your stay in Bali, a toll pass might be a convenient option.
  4. Check traffic updates: Use your preferred navigation app or website to check for real-time traffic updates before planning your route.
  5. Enjoy the views: The Bali Mandara Toll Road offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and the ocean. Take the time to appreciate the scenery while driving.

Conclusion

The Bali Mandara Toll Road is an essential part of the island's infrastructure, offering convenient access to various destinations. Knowing the current pricing structure and having a basic understanding of the toll road systems will ensure a smooth and enjoyable journey for your trip to Bali. Follow these simple tips to make the most out of your experience and have a great time exploring the wonders of Bali!
